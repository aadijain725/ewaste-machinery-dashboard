1. Dismantling Tables MUST HAVE
Rs 55K each (JewelTech) / Rs 3.8L set (Purva)
4'×6' workstations with suction hoods connected to cyclone dust collector, 3m+ stack above roof. Workers manually disassemble e-waste here.

What happens at this stage

Workers use hand tools (screwdrivers, pliers, pneumatic drills) to separate an item into fractions: PCBs, plastics, metals, cables, batteries, hazardous parts. This is where 100% of incoming material first gets processed. A 5-table setup with 10 workers can dismantle ~1 tonne/day of mixed e-waste.

Why suction hoods matter: CPCB inspectors specifically check for dust extraction. Without it, you fail the CTE inspection. The cyclone collects fine dust (lead, cadmium particles from PCBs) before venting through the stack.

Specs (JewelTech)

Size: 4' × 6' per table
Weight: ~210 kg each
All tables connect to shared suction pipe
Cyclone + 3m stack required (separate purchase)
Ask dealer: Does Rs 55K include hood? Cyclone separate?
You need: 5 tables minimum for 1T/day

2. Shredder MUST HAVE
Rs 8-15L (Indian) / Rs 5.5L (JewelTech listed)
Twin-shaft or quad-shaft cutter that reduces dismantled fractions to 10-25mm pieces. This is the bottleneck machine — its throughput determines your CTO capacity.

How it works

Two counter-rotating shafts with interlocking blades grip and tear material. E-waste feeds from a hopper on top, shredded output drops below onto a conveyor. Output particle size is controlled by a screen mesh underneath — smaller mesh = finer output but slower throughput.

Critical nuance: Rated capacity is PEAK. Actual sustained throughput is typically 60-80% of rated. A "200 kg/hr" shredder really does 120-160 kg/hr with mixed e-waste. Always ask for actual throughput data from existing installations.

3. Magnetic Separator MUST HAVE
Rs 2-4L
Overband magnet mounted above conveyor belt. Pulls out ferrous metals (iron, steel) from the shredded stream. First separation stage after shredding.

How it works

A powerful permanent magnet (ferrite or rare earth) sits inside a belt that runs perpendicular to the material conveyor. As shredded material passes underneath, ferrous pieces leap up and stick to the belt, which carries them sideways into a collection bin. Non-ferrous material continues forward on the main conveyor.

What it catches: Steel chassis pieces, iron screws/bolts, ferrous motor housings. This is your Fe certificate stream. E-waste has 5-30% ferrous content depending on category (LSEEW highest at ~25%).

JewelTech Specs

Type: Ferrite or Rare Earth magnet
Belt: Silicone & Rubber
Belt width: 400-800mm
Power: 1-2 kW (very low)
Operating height: up to 2 ft
Maintenance: Very low — magnets last 5-10 years
Rated by: Belt width × belt speed × material depth

4. Density Separator MUST HAVE
Rs 2-5L
Air gravity table that separates materials by density — heavy metals sink, light plastics float. Separates the remaining stream after magnetic and eddy current separation.

How it works

A vibrating inclined table with air blowing upward through tiny holes. Heavy materials (copper nuggets, metal fragments) stay on the table surface and travel one direction. Light materials (plastic, fiber) get lifted by the airflow and travel the other direction. Medium-density items go to a middle stream for re-processing.

Why it's non-negotiable: This is how you recover aluminum and copper from the non-ferrous stream. Without it, you're selling mixed non-ferrous scrap at Rs 150-200/kg instead of separated copper at Rs 700/kg.

Production: 100-1000 kg/hr
Power: 3.1-11.1 kW
Weight: 450 kg
Dimensions: 36"×40"×55"
Application: Metal/non-metal/carbonaceous separation
Rated by: Air volume and table area

5. Eddy Current Separator MUST HAVE
Rs 5-12L
Uses rotating magnetic field to repel and eject non-ferrous metals (aluminium, copper, brass) from a mixed stream of plastic and metal. The key machine for Cu/Al certificate generation.

How it works

A high-speed rotor with alternating N-S magnets spins inside a conveyor belt drum. When non-ferrous metals pass over it, the rapidly changing magnetic field induces eddy currents in the metal, creating a temporary opposing magnetic field. This literally LAUNCHES the non-ferrous pieces forward off the conveyor, while plastic/glass/non-conductors simply fall off the end normally.

The physics matters: Aluminium gets ejected furthest (low density, high conductivity), copper less so (heavy but very conductive), stainless steel barely moves. Adjusting rotor speed and belt speed lets you tune the separation. 95%+ ferrous removal efficiency is standard.

Capacity: up to 500 kg/hr
Power: 8 kW
Weight: 1,500 kg
Dimensions: 96"×48"×84"
Variable Frequency Drives for tuning
Application: E-waste, Li-ion battery, metal recycling
Ask dealer: Rotor specs, belt width, rated kg/hr, separation purity %

6. Conveyors MUST HAVE
Rs 3-6L (set)
Feed conveyors from shredder to separators, sorting conveyors for manual picking, and transfer conveyors between machines. The connective tissue of the plant.

You typically need 3-5 conveyor sections: (1) Feed to shredder, (2) Shredder output to magnetic separator, (3) Post-magnetic to eddy current, (4) Sorting/picking conveyor for manual quality check. Belt width should match your separator belt widths (typically 400-800mm). Speed is adjustable. Low power draw (~1-2 kW each).

CPCB inspection note: Material must flow ONE DIRECTION through the plant — intake on one side, dispatch on the other. No backtracking. Your conveyor layout enforces this.

These 4 machines are triggered by CEEW category (fridges, ACs, washing machines) and large LSEEW items. They only cost Rs 4-14L combined but unlock 35%+ of kabadiwala intake. Without them, you reject most of what walks through your door.
7. Compressor Cutting Machine CEEW TRIGGER
Rs 2-5L
Cuts open sealed hermetic compressors from refrigerators and ACs to extract copper windings, iron body, and oil. Without this, fridge/AC compressors are un-processable scrap.

How it works

A circular saw or hydraulic press cuts the welded steel shell of the compressor in half. Inside you find: copper motor windings (high value, ~1-2 kg per compressor), iron stator, and compressor oil (hazardous, must be recovered). The worker extracts windings manually after cutting.

Economics: A single fridge compressor yields ~1.5 kg copper (Rs 1,050) + iron body. At Rs 30-50/kg input cost for whole fridges, the compressor alone often pays for the item.

10. CRT Cutting Machine DECLINING RELEVANCE
Rs 8-15L
Hot-wire or diamond saw system to cut CRT (cathode ray tube) glass from old TVs and monitors. Separates leaded funnel glass from unleaded panel glass.

How it works

A nichrome wire is heated and wrapped around the CRT at the junction of the funnel and panel glass. Thermal shock cracks the glass cleanly along the wire. The two halves are separated — panel glass (recyclable, lead-free) and funnel glass (contains 20-25% lead oxide, hazardous). The phosphor coating inside is scraped off and treated as hazardous waste.

JewelTech Specs

Power: 2 kW
Capacity: 80-100 units/hour
Weight: 450 kg
Dimensions: 84"×48"
Strategic call: CRT TVs are declining fast. Consider segregating CRTs and sending to another recycler initially rather than buying this machine for Day 1. Save Rs 8-15L upfront.

11. Component Removing Machine (CRM) PCB VALUE UNLOCK
Rs 2-3L (JewelTech: Rs 2.75L)
Heated rotating drum that melts solder to remove components (ICs, capacitors, resistors, connectors) from populated PCBs. Creates bare boards and a component stream.

How it works

PCBs are loaded into a heated drum (~250-300°C). As the drum rotates, solder melts and components fall off through perforations. You get two outputs: (1) bare PCBs (high copper content, easier to shred and process), and (2) loose components (ICs contain gold-plated pins, capacitors contain palladium). The bare boards then go to the shredder → separator line.

Value add: Separated components can be sold to precious metal refiners at Rs 400-800/kg depending on type. The bare boards go through your mechanical line for copper recovery.

13. Data Destruction (Degausser + HDD Crusher) MUST HAVE for ITEW
Rs 3-5L (degausser) + Rs 2.75-6L (crusher/wiper)
Mandatory for processing any IT equipment. Degausser erases magnetic media, physical crusher destroys hard drives. Corporate clients require certificates of destruction.

Degausser: Generates a powerful magnetic pulse that scrambles all data on magnetic media (HDDs, tapes). Takes ~10 seconds per drive. Cannot work on SSDs.
HDD Crusher: Physical destruction — hydraulic press punches through the platters or shreds the entire drive. Works on both HDDs and SSDs.
Data Wiping Software: For drives you want to resell as refurbished — software overwrites all data per DoD/NIST standards. Generates a wipe certificate per drive.

Revenue opportunity: Corporate IT disposal services charge Rs 50-200 per device for certified data destruction. If you process 1,000 corporate drives/month, that's Rs 50K-2L/month in data destruction fees ALONE, before any material recovery.

Pollution control: Furnace operations REQUIRE upgraded APCD — 30m stack, scrubber, carbon filter. This alone can cost Rs 15-30L. Factor this into the decision to add a furnace.

15. Precious Metal Recovery System STAGE 2 — THE ENDGAME
Rs 5.75L (JewelTech) + Rs 10-25L (hydromet) + Rs 5-12L (electrowinning)
Pyro/hydro/electrometallurgical system for extracting gold, silver, palladium from PCBs. Only ~11 recyclers in India have this. The ultimate margin differentiator.

The process chain:
1. Smelting — PCBs are melted in furnace to create a copper-rich matte containing precious metals
2. Chemical Leaching — Aqua regia or cyanide solution dissolves gold/silver from the matte
3. Precipitation — Chemical agents selectively precipitate gold out of solution
4. Electrowinning — Electric current plates pure gold/silver onto cathodes
5. Refining — Final purification to 99.5%+ purity

JewelTech Gold Extraction Machine (Rs 4.15L): Ask exactly what this covers. Likely just the leaching tank + basic precipitation. Full system needs: furnace (Rs 5-15L) + hydromet setup (Rs 10-25L) + electrowinning cells (Rs 5-12L) + upgraded APCD with scrubber + carbon filter (Rs 15-30L) + ETP upgrade to 5 KLD ZLD (Rs 10-20L) + fume hoods + lab equipment (Rs 3-8L).

Total for gold capability: Rs 45L-1.25 Cr additional over Config 2 base. Justifiable when you have 500+ kg/month of PCBs from corporate sources. At 0.0004% gold recovery from PCBs, 1 tonne of server PCBs yields ~4g gold = Rs 30,000 in gold alone + the copper underneath.

18. Air Pollution Control Devices (APCD) CRITICAL COMPLIANCE
Rs 4-8L (basic) / Rs 15-30L (with furnace)
Cyclone + bag house + alkaline scrubber + off-gas treatment + stack. Captures dust, fumes, and hazardous particles from all processing operations.

Basic APCD (no furnace): Cyclone dust collector (from dismantling tables) + bag filter (from shredder area) + 3m stack above roof. Rs 4-8L.
Full APCD (with furnace): Everything above + alkaline scrubber + activated carbon filter + 30m stack. Rs 15-30L. The 30m stack alone can cost Rs 3-5L.
CPCB inspection: They check stack height, emission monitoring port, and may take air quality readings. This is not optional — it's the #1 reason facilities fail CTE inspection.

19. ETP (Effluent Treatment Plant) CRITICAL COMPLIANCE
Rs 5-10L (2 KLD) / Rs 10-20L (5 KLD ZLD)
Treats wastewater from processing (acid wash, cooling water, floor wash). Capacity in KLD (kilolitres/day). Must be adequate for your operations.

For Config 2 (no furnace): 2 KLD ETP with sludge drying bed is adequate. Handles floor wash water, dust suppression runoff, and minor process water. Rs 5-10L.
For Config 4 (with gold recovery): 5 KLD with ZLD (Zero Liquid Discharge) preferred. Hydromet operations generate acid-contaminated wastewater that needs neutralization, precipitation, and evaporation. Rs 10-20L.
Sludge: ETP sludge is hazardous waste — must go to authorized TSDF with manifests.

20. Weighbridge MUST HAVE
Rs 2-4L
Platform scale at intake zone to weigh incoming vehicles/material. Required for material flow tracking and EPR portal reporting.

Typically 10-30 tonne capacity. At 500 sqm, you likely use a platform scale (3m×3m) rather than a full truck weighbridge. Every incoming lot must be weighed, photographed, and lot-numbered. This data feeds into your material balance reporting on the EPR portal. CPCB/SPCB auditors verify material balance: input weight must approximately equal output weights across all fractions.

21. Weighing Machines / Equipment MUST HAVE
Rs 0.5-1L
Bench scales and floor scales at each processing stage for material flow tracking. Essential for EPR compliance and material balance reporting.

You need scales at: intake, post-dismantling (per fraction), post-shredding, post-separation (per metal), output/dispatch, and hazardous waste storage. Typically 3-5 bench scales (50-100 kg capacity) + 1-2 floor scales (500-1000 kg capacity). Digital with printout capability preferred for audit trail.

7. Key Questions for Ghaziabad Visit — Priority Order

Understanding What You're Actually Buying

Q1. For a 330 TPA CTO (1T/day, 8hrs), what SPECIFIC machines and specs do you recommend? Give me a complete bill of materials with individual prices.

Q2. What is the ACTUAL sustained throughput (not rated) of your shredder in kg/hr for mixed e-waste? Can you show data from an existing installation?

Q3. Which of the 21 CPCB Annexure 1 items do you NOT supply? (Likely: ETP, weighbridge, 30m furnace stack)

Q4. Do you supply a complete turnkey solution — layout planning + installation + commissioning + SPCB documentation support? Or just machines?

Q5. Which recycler clients of yours recently got CTO from Haryana/UP SPCB using your machines? Can I call them or visit their plant?

Space & Fit

Q6. I have two 500 sqm plots. Can you design a layout for Config 2 that works? What's the minimum covered area needed for the mechanical line?

Q7. Ceiling height requirements — dismantling needs 3m+, shredder needs crane access (4-5m). Can this work in a standard industrial shed?

9. Decision Framework for Your Visit

Decide BEFORE the visit

1. Config 2 is the play. Don't waste time on Config 1 (won't get recycler CTO) or Config 4 (too early for gold). Focus entirely on getting the best Config 2 quotes from both Purva and JewelTech.

2. Space decision. If your two 500 sqm plots can be used together, Config 2 fits with room to spare. If not, you're renting. Know your max monthly rent budget before discussing layout.

3. Indian vs Chinese mechanical line. Chinese integrated line (Rs 12-38L landed) vs Indian individual machines (Rs 25-45L for same capability). The Chinese line is cheaper and matched-throughput, but spare parts take 6-8 weeks and after-sales is remote. Indian gives you local support, especially from Purva in Sahibabad.

Decide AFTER the visit

1. Actual prices. IndiaMART listings are inflated. Get real quotes with all ancillaries listed (installation, commissioning, conveyors, ducting — often "extra").

2. Reference calls. Get 2-3 recycler references who are actually operating with their machines. Call them. Ask: actual throughput, breakdown frequency, SPCB inspection experience, what they wish they'd known.

3. Timeline. Delivery + installation + commissioning. If it takes 3+ months, factor that into your CTE application timeline. You need machines installed before SPCB inspects for CTO.

4. Gold recovery reality. JewelTech's Gold Extraction (Rs 4.15L) and Precious Metal Refining (Rs 5.75L) — understand what's REALLY included. If it's just a leaching tank and a basic precipitation setup, the total gold pathway is still Rs 45L+.
